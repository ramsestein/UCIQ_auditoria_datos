CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. SIGNAL PREVALENCE: UCIQ demonstrates more comprehensive monitoring with higher
   prevalence of hemodynamic (ABP, CVP, PAP), neurological (ICP, BIS), and
   ventilatory signals, while MIMIC focuses on basic physiological monitoring.

2. RECORDING CHARACTERISTICS: MIMIC has significantly longer recordings suitable
   for longitudinal analysis, while UCIQ has shorter, more standardized captures.

3. MONITORING PHENOTYPES: Six distinct phenotypes identified, with UCIQ showing
   higher diversity and more comprehensive monitoring configurations.

4. DOMAIN SHIFT: Substantial differences in signal composition create a domain
   shift that limits direct model transferability between datasets.

5. ROBUSTNESS: 75% of identified differences are robust to sampling variation,
   indicating genuine practice differences rather than sampling artifacts.

RECOMMENDATIONS:
• Use dataset-specific models or implement domain adaptation
• Consider merging both datasets for more robust training
• Account for monitoring phenotype when selecting records for specific analyses
